Tracking Parameters Explained

No panic. No jargon fog. Just the essentials.

Tracking parameters are URL additions, usually after ?. Common examples: utm_source, fbclid, gclid. They can help campaign analytics, but are often unnecessary when forwarding links.

What are they exactly?

Base URL

https://example.com/article

With parameters

https://example.com/article?utm_source=newsletter&utm_campaign=launch&fbclid=XYZ

The page can be the same — only the URL attachment differs.

When to remove, when to keep

Often good to remove

  • Forwarding to friends/teams
  • Documentation, wikis, notes
  • Posts, comments, chats

Sometimes better to keep

  • Affiliate/partner links (ref, tag)
  • Functional keys (language, filters, state)
  • Intentional campaign measurement in your own context
Important: link cleaning is a hygiene step, not an invisibility promise.

Practical flow

  1. Copy link
  2. Paste into SafeShare
  3. Clean and share intentionally
