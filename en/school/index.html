What do these “labels” look like?

https://example.com/article?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=launch
https://shop.example.com/product?gclid=ABC123&fbclid=XYZ987
utm_* campaigns

Shows: source/medium/campaign. Useful for analytics—often unnecessary when sharing.

gclid / fbclid click IDs

Platform-specific IDs for measurement. When sharing, usually just ballast.

Privacy doesn’t mean “being invisible.” It means: create less data, share less data, keep more control.

Why should a non-technical person do this?

  • Respect: you pass on less extra info others didn’t choose.
  • Looks cleaner: links feel more trustworthy and less “ad-like.”
  • Less breakage: some apps shorten/rewrite long URLs in unhelpful ways.
Important: this is not a promise of “invisibility.” It’s a concrete hygiene step when sharing links.

When should you NOT remove everything blindly?

Affiliate/partners

Some parameters are attribution (e.g., ref, tag). Removing them can break tracking/compensation.

Pro option: allowlist per domain/policy.

Functional parameters

Some keys are required (language, product ID, session, filters). Good tools are conservative and don’t remove randomly.

App: standard tracking. Pro: audit + rules.
