Remove UTM parameters

UTM parameters (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign …) are extra bits of information on a link — like labels. Useful for analytics, but often just ballast when sharing.

Clean now School Pro (rules/audit)
SafeShare works local-first: links are cleaned in your browser, not uploaded.

Example

Before: https://example.com/article?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=launch
After: https://example.com/article

The most important UTM keys

utm_source – source (e.g., twitter, newsletter)

utm_medium – channel (e.g., social, email)

utm_campaign – campaign

utm_term – search term/keyword (optional)

utm_content – variants/A/B (optional)

utm_id – campaign ID (optional)

When is removing UTM useful?

  • You share links privately or publicly and want to pass along fewer “labels”.
  • You want links to look clean (e.g., publishing, editorial, school).
  • You want to avoid additional click IDs (e.g., gclid/fbclid) tagging along.

When not to remove blindly

Affiliate/partner parameters (e.g., ref, tag) can control attribution/revenue. If you’re responsible for that: use Pro (whitelist/allowlist + audit).

How to do it with SafeShare

  1. Copy the link
  2. Paste it into the SafeShare App
  3. Clean → copy → share
