Clean Links in Emails (Outlook, Gmail, Newsletters)

Email is an archive: links end up in inboxes, forwards, CRMs, tickets, and backups. If tracking IDs are attached (utm_*, click IDs), they become part of that trail—often unintentionally.

Quick Workflows

Copy → Clean → Paste (single link)

  1. Copy the link from a site/tool.
  2. Open SafeShare → paste → clean.
  3. Paste the clean link into your email.

Multiple links in one email (Pro)

  1. Prepare a draft with multiple URLs.
  2. Review changes in Pro (Audit).
  3. Apply a consistent policy and send.

Important limit (honest)

Don’t remove everything blindly: parameters like ref or tag may be intentionally used for attribution.

Use Pro policies (allowlists/rules per domain) when you need fine control.

Why this reduces data spread

Less metadata in tool chains

Forwards, ticket systems, and internal tools often store full URLs. Removing tracking params reduces extra context leaking into these systems.

Cleaner team hygiene

Clean URLs are easier to read, more professional, and reduce accidental campaign-ID leakage in daily workflows.
